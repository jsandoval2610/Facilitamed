The problem

A difficult financial situation poses a threat to students who are trying to finish their studies. This problem is aggravated if these students play an important role in the family´s economy.

After completing a high school education, teenager’s expectations at home rise to becoming a provider for their family, shifting their desire for a college education to a secondary priority. The added responsibility and the constant financial worries cause teenagers to restart the cycle of financial difficulties for them and their future generations.

We want to break this cycle!

How?

We have identified a number of students who are currently undergoing socio-economic struggles and we are providing the necessary resources for them to better focus on their studies. These students are currently in medicine school and have a successful track record of grades, attendance, and participation. Nevertheless, they have demonstrated how their personal situation has derailed that success.

We aim to provide the resources on a monthly basis and provide mentoring resources to ensure this student gets back on track to succeed in their career. They, in return have to present a plan on how they expect to utilize our resources.

In Guatemala there are 9.7 doctors for every 10,000 habitants. Total population 16.7m (2019). The number has been decreasing in the last 10 years.

In El Salvador there are 15.7 doctors for every 10,000 habitants, this has declined from 19.2 in 2008. Total population 6.45m (2019).

In Nicaragua there are 10.1 doctors for every 10,000 habitants. Total population 6.5m (2019).

In countries like Germany, Spain and Switzerland, there are at least 38 doctors for every 10,000 inhabitants.
